Independent Study on Due Process in Algorithmic System

I work with Prof. Malte Zeiwitz of Cornell's Department of Science and Technology Studies to probe the question of due process in algorithmic systems.

In the last few years, algorithmic decisions have quickly become commonplace.Hiring managers use algorithms to sort through resumes and find the best candidate for a job; credit scoring algorithms track people’s payment history to assign them a low or high credit score; and predictive policing uses data to identify areas of potentially high crime

However, most companies keep their algorithms secret, so not even experts are allowed to peek inside the “black box.” So what can someone who believes he/she has been wrongfully classified, rejected or policed by an algorithm do to challenge the decision? This is the question I am exploring.
